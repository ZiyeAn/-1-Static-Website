Caps Pileus

What is pileus?

The shape of the pileus (the cap) of mushrooms is one of the key features used in mushroom identification. Different species exhibit different cap shapes, and understanding these variations helps in distinguishing between types. Here are some common pileus shapes and their importance in mushroom classification.

Convex:
• Dome-shaped, with the top rounded outwards.
• Common in many young mushrooms and slowly flattens with age.
• Example: Many Agaricus species, like the common button mushroom.

Campanulate:
• Shaped like a bell with a rounded top and edges that curve downward.
• Often seen in younger mushrooms of certain species.
• Example: Mycena species.

Umbonate:
• Convex with a pronounced central bump (called an umbo).
• The umbo can help separate species within the same genus.
• Example: Marasmius oreades (fairy ring mushroom).

Flat (or Plane):
• Fully expanded and flat across the top.
• Usually seen in mature mushrooms as the cap flattens with age.
• Example: Mature Amanita muscaria.

Depressed:
• Center of the cap is sunken or concave.
• Useful in distinguishing certain species that may otherwise look similar to convex types.
• Example: Chanterelle (Cantharellus spp.).
